What is the moon made of?more_vert

The Moon: NASA

Spoiler alert: it's not cheese.close

The moon is made primarily of rock and metal, just like planet Earth. The moon's surface is heavily cratered and is littered with dead volcanoes, as well as ancient lava flows--some of which can be seen with the naked eye. Beneath the surface is a potentially solid iron core, surrounded by an intermediate layer of molten liquid iron.

Because of Tidal Locking.close

The moon orbits around Earth every 28 days, and the moon rotates completely around its axis in 28 days. The moon and the Earth exert force on each other, and the force that is exerted between these two celestial bodies is stronger where they face each other.
