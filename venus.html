Venus Overview: NASA

It's mostly carbon dioxide.close

Venus has a very thick and toxic atmosphere which means the planet is constantly covered in a yellowish shroud of sulfuric acid clouds. This causes a runaway greenhouse effect making Venus the hottest planet in our solar system despite not being the closest body to the sun. Its surface has an extreme pressure gradient that would be similar to the pressure felt a mile below the surface of the ocean on Earth. The atmosphere on Venus is also rotating extremely quickly while the planet is rotating much more slowly. The hurricane force winds blow the clouds all the way around the Earth sized planet every 5 days.

Venus
  • Mass: 4.867 x 10^24 kg
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
  • Average distance from Earth: 42 million miles
  • Average distance from the Sun: 108 million miles

Venus Exploration: NASA

Venus has been heavily explored.close

Over 40 spacecraft have visited Venus to explore the super-hot planet. In the 90's, the Magellan mission mapped 98% of the surface of Venus, and the Akatsuki probe from Japan is currently orbiting it for exploration purposes. In our extensive studies of this planet, we have discovered that Venus periodically passes in front of the sun in a process called transit. These transits occur in pairs and are separated by over a century each time. These long spaced transit pairs help us to study Venus and its place in the solar system.
