Saturn's Rings: NASA

Predominately rock & ice.close

Saturn's rings were first observed by Galileo in 1610, the same year he discovered Jupiter's moons. These brilliant rings are made up of rock and ice particles, broken off of various objects floating in space and pulled into Saturn's orbit by its powerful gravitational field. The size of the particles that comprise the rings range in size from smaller than a grain of sand, all the way up to the size of mountains on Earth. Just outside of the brilliant main rings of this giant planet, is Saturn's E-ring. This diffuse ring is fed by water jets spouting from Enceladus, one of Saturn's many moons.

Saturn Overview: NASA

What type of planet is Saturn?close

While Earth is one of four terrestrial (or, rocky) planets in our solar system, Saturn is one of four Gas Giants along with Jupiter, Uranus and Neptune. Saturn does not have a solid surface like Earth, and is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium, or, very hot air. There may be a solid core somewhere inside of Saturn's gaseous form, but Earthlings have yet to reach far enough inside the hot clouds to find out. Saturn is also one of the largest planets in our star system and about two tons of its mass comes from Earth. In 2017, the Cassini spacecraft (an orbiter) was intentionally vaporized into Saturn's atmosphere making it a permanent part the "Jewel of the Universe."
