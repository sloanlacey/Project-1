How fast is Mercury moving?more_vert

Mercury: NASA

Very, very fast.close

Mercury is the fastest moving planet in out solar system, and is appropriately named after Mercurius, the Roman God of speed. Mercury orbits the sun every 88 Earth days, and is moving at an average speed of 29 miles per second. However, while Mercurian years are very short, the days on Mercury are extremely long. The day-night cycle of Mercury takes just shy of 176 Earth days.

Mercury at a Glance
  • Mass: 3.285 x 10^23 kg
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Average distance from Earth: 48 million miles
  • Distance from the Sun: 41.553 million miles

Mercury's Hollows: NASA

What are the hollows on Mercury?close

NASA's Messenger spacecraft discovered unexpected hollows on the surface of Mercury at a varierty of locations. The hollows range in depth from 60 ft. to 120 ft. and are considered mysterious as no one knows how they got there. In the years we have been observing the terrestrial planet, we have discovered that Mercury's surface doesn't change much with the exception of impact cratering. The most fascinating aspect of the appearance of these hollows is that it means that Mercury's surface is changing in a way we didn't expect.
