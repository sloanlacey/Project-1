What's in Jupiter's red spot?more_vert

Mars: NASA

A storm.close

Jupiter's iconic giant red spot is actually a long raging storm. It is currently about 1.3 times as wide as Earth, and its counter-clockwise winds far exceed any wind speeds recorded on our home planet. The storm was first observed in 1831 but is likely much, much older.

How many moons does Jupiter have?close

Jupiter has 79 confirmed moons. Its first four--Europa, Io, Ganymede, and Callisto--are of the greatest scientific interest due to their compositions. These four moons are known as the Galilean Satellites.
