Is Neptune's ice like ours?more_vert

"Icy" Neptune: NASA

There is ice, but it's different from ice on Earth.close

Neptune is one of the four giant planets in our solar system, and it is one of only two Ice Giants (the other is its neighbor Uranus). In planetary science, the term "ice" refers to volatile chemical compounds with freezing points above 100 Kelvin (about -280 degrees Fahrenheit). There is a small amount of water ice on Neptune, but the majority of its ice is in the form of methane and ammonia. Its surface is a layer of hot, dense fluid with icy clouds all wrapped around smaller, rocky core. Scientists speculate that beneath the icy clouds is an ocean of extremely hot water.

Neptune
  • Mass: 1.024 x 10^26 kg
  • Orbital Period: 165 Earth years
  • Average distance from Earth: 2.7 billion miles
  • Average distance from the Sun: 2.8 billion miles
Extreme Neptune Windsmore_vert

Neptune's Winds: NASA

Neptune's winds are 9 times as strong as the winds on Earth.close

Yep, you read that right. The wind speeds on Neptune are also 3 times as fast as those on Jupiter. Despite its low energy input from the sun, Neptune is the windiest planet in our solar system with speeds of more than 1,200 miles per hour. The winds blow the clouds of frozen methane around the planet at extremely high speeds as it makes its 165 Earth-year long journey around the sun. Neptune's atmosphere, along with the small amount of methane, is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium. In comparison, Uranus has a very similar atmosphere but Neptune is a much more vivid blue. This means there is some unknown component that provides its more intense color.
