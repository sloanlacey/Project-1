Is Pluto a planet?more_vert

Pluto: NASA

It's complicated.close

Pluto's status as a planet (or, not as a planet) has been up for debate over the years, especially since 2006. This is primarily due to Pluto not meeting the planetary criteria of the International Astronomical Union. A consideration of planets by the IAU that Pluto does not meet is in its orbital path. Typical planets clear the area around their orbit of other objects floating nearby, something Pluto has yet to do. While it falls short of the criteria to make it a bona fide planet, it does meet the criteria for a dwarf planet.

Pluto
  • Mass: 1.024 x 10^26 kg
  • Orbital Period: 249 Earth years
  • Average distance from Earth: 3.3 billion miles
  • Average distance from the Sun: 3.7 billion miles
What is Pluto's atmosphere like?more_vert

Pluto's Hazy Atmosphere: NASA

Thin and hazy.close

The dwarf planet Pluto is smaller than Earth's moon, and has brilliant and beautiful features to study. It boasts a heart-shaped glacier the size of Texas and Oklahoma combined, blue skies, moons that spin, mountains as high as the Rockies, and red snow. Pluto's atmosphere is also an interesting study as it is quite thin and very hazy. The atmosphere on Pluto expands as it gets closer to the sun, and collapses as it gets farther away--much like a comet. As it approaches the sun, Pluto's surface ice turns directly from solid to gas (sublimation) and rises to form the thin atmosphere. As it moves away from the sun, most of the gas freezes solid again. Its main atmospheric ingredient is nitrogen, but molecules of carbon dioxide and methane have also been detected.
